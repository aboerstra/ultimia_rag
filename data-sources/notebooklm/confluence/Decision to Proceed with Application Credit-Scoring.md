Personal Credit Report Processing</strong></h2><ul><li><p>Personal Credit Reports are ran via LaserCreditAccess Integration and provide credit reports on the Contacts that will be assessed for financing as part of the deal.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>1.1 Pulling the Credit Report</strong></h3><ul><li><p>In Salesforce, enter in required information prior to pulling a credit report for a Contact (SSN, full address, birthdate).</p></li><li><p><strong>Click relevant field on the Contact record to pull a credit report</strong>.</p></li><li><p>Options:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Pull Credit Report - Experian</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Pull Credit Report - TransUnion (no longer used)</strong></p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>1.2 Upload and Save Credit Report</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Wait <strong>10-15 seconds</strong> before refreshing the page to view the report.</p></li><li><p>Download the credit report.</p></li><li><p>If the file is locked, <strong>print it to PDF</strong> and overwrite the original file.</p></li><li><p>The credit report is <strong>downloaded and saved</strong>.</p></li><li><p>If the file is <strong>locked</strong>, it is printed to <strong>PDF format</strong> and the original file is overwritten to ensure accessibility.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>2. Manual Credit Check (Performed by the Credit Team)</strong></h2><h3><strong>2.1 Identifying &ldquo;Non-Starters&rdquo; (Manual Review)</strong></h3><p>After retrieving the credit report, the <strong>credit team manually reviews</strong> it to identify &ldquo;non-starters,&rdquo; which are <strong>automatic disqualifiers</strong> for financing.</p><h3><strong>2.2 Non-Starter Criteria (Manual Review Triggers Immediate Decline)</strong></h3><p>If any of the following conditions are met, the <strong>application is declined immediately</strong>:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Auto charge-offs within the last five years</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Charge-offs greater than $10,000</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Significant child support delinquency</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Dismissed bankruptcy</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Judgments on file</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Collections</strong></p></li></ul><p>This <strong>manual review</strong> ensures applications with severe credit issues are screened before continuing the decision process.</p><p><ac:emoticon ac:name="warning" ac:emoji-shortname=":warning:" ac:emoji-id="atlassian-warning" ac:emoji-fallback=":warning:" /> Laura to check if this is currently still done and if so, in what capacity</p><hr /><h2><strong>3. Scoring the Deal</strong></h2><ul><li><p>Scoring calculations are ran via KinAnalytics model/integration and use the personal credit reports and details from the Opportunity and Collateral (Vehicle) in order to provide back a Scoring recommendation to the user based on the model.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>3.1 Calculating the Score </strong></h3><ul><li><p>On the Opportunity, User clicks <strong>&quot;Calculate Score&quot;</strong> which initiates the integration with KinAnalytics.</p></li><li><p>The system processes <strong>all relevant credit and financial data</strong> and generates a score.</p></li><li><p>A Scoring Record is created against the Opportunity each time the Scoring is ran.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>3.2 Objective Decision-Making</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The <strong>KIN Model Scoring System</strong> provides a structured, data-driven approach to evaluating applicants.</p></li><li><p>It references multiple <strong>data sources</strong>, including:</p><ul><li><p>XML credit report data</p></li><li><p>User-entered application data</p></li><li><p>Proprietary internal scoring algorithms</p></li></ul></li></ul><p>This system ensures <strong>consistent, objective credit decisions</strong> while reducing manual evaluation errors.</p><hr /><h2><strong>4. Determining Down Payment and Rates</strong></h2><h3><strong>4.1 Down Payment Calculation</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The credit team determines the <strong>required down payment</strong> based on the applicant&rsquo;s credit profile.</p></li><li><p>The <strong>down payment may be adjusted</strong> based on deal specifics, such as vehicle type and applicant history.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>4.2 Rate Calculation</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The interest rate provided to the customer is based on their <strong>credit profile and lending score</strong>.</p></li><li><p>The <strong>&quot;T Value Calculator&quot;</strong> is used to determine lending terms.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>4.3 T Value Calculator Limitations</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The <strong>T Value Calculator</strong> does <strong>not</strong> automatically adjust for changes in fees.</p></li><li><p>If fees increase or decrease, a <strong>manual recalculation</strong> is performed to compare the system-generated terms with the adjusted fees.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>5. System-Identified Non-Starters (Automated Review)</strong></h2><h3><strong>5.1 System Notifications (System Flags Potential Issues)</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The system <strong>automatically flags applicants</strong> if their credit profile suggests a high likelihood of decline.</p></li><li><p>This notification <strong>does not replace the manual review</strong> but serves as an additional <strong>early warning</strong>.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>5.2 Manual vs. System-Based Non-Starter Checks</strong></h3><table data-table-width="760" data-layout="default" ac:local-id="965e2c3d-f78b-42ed-ba47-128e0a3a00f9"><tbody><tr><th><p><strong>Process Type</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Who Performs It?</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Purpose</strong></p></th><th><p><strong>Outcome</strong></p></th></tr><tr><td><p><strong>Manual Credit Check</strong></p></td><td><p>Credit team (Human Review)</p></td><td><p>Identifies major financial red flags</p></td><td><p>Immediate decline if criteria met</p></td></tr><tr><td><p><strong>System-Based Non-Starter Check</strong></p></td><td><p>Automated system in SFDC</p></td><td><p>Provides early warnings on credit risks</p></td><td><p>Not an immediate decline; requires manual review</p></td></tr></tbody></table><p>The <strong>manual check takes priority</strong> over the system-generated warnings, as it involves <strong>human judgment</strong> based on experience.</p><hr /><h3><strong>Summary of Key Distinctions</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Manual Credit Check</strong> = Performed by the credit team; <strong>immediate declines</strong> occur if non-starter conditions are met.</p></li><li><p><strong>System-Based Non-Starter Check</strong> = <strong>Automated warnings</strong> that highlight potential risks but do not make final decisions.</p></li><li><p><strong>The KIN Model Scoring System</strong> helps refine <strong>objective</strong> credit decisions.</p></li><li><p><strong>Down payment and rate determination</strong> rely on both <strong>manual adjustments and system calculations</strong>.</p></li></ul><p>This ensures a <strong>balanced approach</strong> to credit decision-making, reducing risk while maximizing approved opportunities.</p><hr /><p />
