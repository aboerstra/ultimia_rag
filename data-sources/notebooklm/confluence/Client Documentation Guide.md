<p /><h2><strong>Introduction</strong></h2><p>This guide outlines the structure and key elements required to create and maintain comprehensive documentation for any client. The documentation serves as a foundational resource for internal teams, Faye employees, and AI training to provide accurate and consistent support. This guide ensures that researchers and documentation specialists produce high-quality, standardized content, following best practices methodologies where applicable.</p><h2><strong>1. Service Blueprint</strong></h2><h3><strong>Purpose:</strong></h3><p>To map out the client&rsquo;s end-to-end business processes, identifying key touchpoints, workflows, and automation opportunities.</p><h3><strong>Best Practices Reference:</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Nielsen Norman Group (NNG) Usability Principles for Service Design</p></li><li><p>Business Process Model and Notation (BPMN) for workflow mapping</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Recommended Visuals:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Process Flow Diagrams</strong> (BPMN for process steps, decision points, and hand-offs)</p></li><li><p><strong>Swimlane Diagrams</strong> (for visualizing roles and responsibilities in workflows)</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Contents:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Customer Journey</strong>: Step-by-step breakdown from application intake to funding.</p></li><li><p><strong>Internal Workflow</strong>: How teams process and approve deals.</p></li><li><p><strong>Pain Points &amp; Opportunities</strong>: Identified bottlenecks and potential process improvements.</p></li><li><p><strong>Role-Based Responsibilities</strong>: Who is responsible for each step in the process.</p></li><li><p><strong>Automation Considerations</strong>: Areas where technology can streamline operations.</p></li></ul><h2><strong>2. Executive Overview</strong></h2><h3><strong>Purpose:</strong></h3><p>To provide high-level insight into the client&rsquo;s business, leadership, and strategic positioning.</p><h3><strong>Best Practices Reference:</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Harvard Business Review (HBR) on Competitive Strategy and Differentiation</p></li><li><p>Porter&rsquo;s Five Forces Analysis for Market Positioning</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Recommended Visuals:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Organizational Chart</strong> (showing leadership structure and departmental breakdown)</p></li><li><p><strong>Competitive Analysis Table</strong> (comparison of key competitors across market position, strengths, and weaknesses)</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Contents:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Company Background</strong>: Mission, history, and evolution.</p></li><li><p><strong>Organizational Structure</strong>: Departments and key leadership roles.</p></li><li><p><strong>Competitive Landscape</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>Major competitors and differentiators.</p></li><li><p>Strengths, weaknesses, and market positioning.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>Revenue Model</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>How the client generates income (loans, leases, brokerage fees, etc.).</p></li><li><p>Cost structure and key financial considerations.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>Future Growth Strategy</strong>: Expansion plans and anticipated market changes.</p></li></ul><h2><strong>3. Document Process (User Manual)</strong></h2><h3><strong>Purpose:</strong></h3><p>To detail how internal users manage documentation across business lines.</p><h3><strong>Best Practices Reference:</strong></h3><ul><li><p>ISO 9001 Documentation Standards</p></li><li><p>UX Writing Guidelines for Clarity and Readability</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Recommended Visuals:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Step-by-Step Flowcharts</strong> (for document processing workflows)</p></li><li><p><strong>Tables for Standardized Data</strong> (document types, required fields, validation rules)</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Contents:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Application Intake Process</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>Channels (email, web forms, broker submissions, etc.).</p></li><li><p>Common rejection reasons.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>Approval &amp; Underwriting</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>Credit Scoring Process.</p></li><li><p>Contingencies and additional document requests.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>Funding &amp; Booking Procedures</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>Steps to finalize funding and move to &quot;Funded&quot; status.</p></li><li><p>Common errors and resolution steps.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>Collections &amp; Asset Management</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>How delinquent accounts are handled.</p></li><li><p>Repossession procedures and resale processes.</p></li></ul></li></ul><h2><strong>4. Technical Architecture</strong></h2><h3><strong>Purpose:</strong></h3><p>To document the client&rsquo;s technology stack, integrations, and system dependencies.</p><h3><strong>Best Practices Reference:</strong></h3><ul><li><p>TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework) for IT Infrastructure</p></li><li><p>ITIL (Information Technology Infrastructure Library) for Service Management</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Recommended Visuals:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>System Architecture Diagrams</strong> (showing how applications interact and integrate)</p></li><li><p><strong>Data Flow Diagrams</strong> (mapping how data moves between systems)</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Contents:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Application Ecosystem</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>Core platforms (CRM, financial systems, document storage, etc.).</p></li><li><p>How data flows between systems.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>System Integrations</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>API connections and automation.</p></li><li><p>Third-party data services.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>CRM Infrastructure</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>Flows, automation, custom fields.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>Document Storage Strategy</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>Data organization and access control.</p></li></ul></li></ul><h2><strong>5. Future State (Strategic Roadmap)</strong></h2><h3><strong>Purpose:</strong></h3><p>To outline the client&rsquo;s technology and process improvement goals.</p><h3><strong>Best Practices Reference:</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Agile Roadmap Planning Frameworks</p></li><li><p>McKinsey Digital Transformation Strategy</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Recommended Visuals:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Roadmap Timelines</strong> (showing implementation phases)</p></li><li><p><strong>Gap Analysis Tables</strong> (current vs. future state comparison)</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Contents:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Planned System Enhancements</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>AI-Assisted Operations</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Process Efficiency Goals</strong></p></li></ul><h2><strong>6. Lines of Business</strong></h2><h3><strong>Purpose:</strong></h3><p>To document distinct business lines, ensuring accurate reference for internal teams and AI training.</p><h3><strong>Best Practices Reference:</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Lean Business Model Canvas</p></li><li><p>Industry-Specific Compliance Standards (e.g., FINRA for financial services)</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Recommended Visuals:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Business Model Canvas</strong> (to document each line&rsquo;s unique strategy)</p></li><li><p><strong>Revenue Breakdown Tables</strong> (by product, service, or customer segment)</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Contents:</strong></h3><p>Each business line should include:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Overview</strong>: Brief introduction to the business line.</p></li><li><p><strong>Target Market</strong>: The industries and customer segments served.</p></li><li><p><strong>Unique Processes</strong>: Key differences in workflows compared to other lines.</p></li><li><p><strong>Key Challenges</strong>: Industry-specific challenges and risk factors.</p></li><li><p><strong>Revenue Model</strong>: Primary sources of income.</p></li><li><p><strong>Technology Stack</strong>: Systems and integrations unique to this business line.</p></li><li><p><strong>Regulatory Considerations</strong>: Compliance requirements and industry regulations.</p></li><li><p><strong>KPIs &amp; Performance Metrics</strong>: Benchmarks for success.</p></li></ul><h2><strong>7. Glossary &amp; Data Dictionary</strong></h2><h3><strong>Recommended Visuals:</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Tables for Key Data Elements</strong> (fields, definitions, sources)</p></li><li><p><strong>Glossary Index</strong> (alphabetized for quick lookup)</p></li></ul><h2><strong>8.
