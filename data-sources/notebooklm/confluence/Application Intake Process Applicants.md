<ac:structured-macro ac:name="toc" ac:schema-version="1" data-layout="default" ac:local-id="bcbaa605-a915-4eb2-b801-b9d2444f73eb" ac:macro-id="a01ccf34-798b-4000-9dae-97b54d5ae96d"><ac:parameter ac:name="minLevel">1</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="maxLevel">2</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="outline">false</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="style">none</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="type">list</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="printable">true</ac:parameter></ac:structured-macro><h2>Explanation of Process &amp; Key Details</h2><p>This is the key process of getting new business - new applications for finance are equivalent to Opportunities/Deals.</p><p>Start to finish, it takes 6-10 minutes to process an intake application (via the emailed process from Dealers/Brokers). </p><p>They get 30-40 applications per day on average.</p><h2><strong>1. Sources of Applications</strong></h2><p /><ul><li><p><strong>(Primary Source) Emails from Brokers and Dealers</strong>: Applications received through email at <a href="mailto:submitted@maximumcc.com"><strong>submit@maximcc.com</strong></a>. </p><ul><li><p>Users monitor inbox for these and then begin process to manually create records in Salesforce in order to process application.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>(Additional Source) Website Submissions</strong>: Applications received internally via Lead/Applicant Qualification which are Qualified then sent to <a href="mailto:submit@maximcc.com">submit@maximcc.com</a> </p></li></ul><h2><strong>2. Initial Review and Verification</strong></h2><p>Each application undergoes an initial review to ensure eligibility.</p><h3><strong>2.1 Opening and Reviewing Attachments</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Review all attachments submitted with the application.</p></li><li><p>Verify the completeness of documents before proceeding to the next steps.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>2.2 Location Check</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Verify if the applicant&rsquo;s location is within Maxim&rsquo;s approved areas.</p></li><li><p><strong>Auto-Decline Locations</strong>: Applications from <strong>Louisiana, Alaska, and Hawaii</strong> are automatically declined.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>2.3 Dealer Verification</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Check if the dealer is <strong>approved and not blacklisted</strong>.</p></li><li><p>Decline applications from previously declined or blacklisted dealers.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>2.4 Equipment Eligibility</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Confirm if the equipment aligns with Maxim&rsquo;s <strong>transportation financing criteria</strong>.</p></li><li><p>If the equipment is <strong>not eligible</strong>, forward the application to the internal team for review.</p></li><li><p>Applications are declined if the equipment does not meet financing requirements.</p></li><li><p><strong>Truck Requirements:</strong></p><ul><li><p>Model year <strong>2020 or newer</strong>.</p></li><li><p>Mileage <strong>less than 700,000 miles</strong>.</p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>2.5 Documentation Handling</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Email Storage</strong>: Convert the email into a <strong>PDF file</strong>, naming it &quot;credit app [Date].&quot;</p></li><li><p><strong>File Storage</strong>: Download and save all attached files.</p></li><li><p><strong>Excel File Conversion</strong>: Convert any Excel files to <strong>PDF format</strong> before storage.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>2.6 Response Time</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Reps must <strong>respond to applications within 1-2 hours</strong> to increase chances of securing business.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>3. Creating Accounts and Contacts in Salesforce &amp; Run Credit Report</strong></h2><p>Once an application passes the initial review, it is logged into <strong>Salesforce (SFDC)</strong>.</p><h3><strong>3.1 Check for Existing Contact</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Search for the applicant's <strong>Social Security Number (SSN)</strong> in Salesforce.</p></li><li><p>If the contact does not exist, create a <strong>new Contact record</strong>.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>3.2 Create an Account for the Customer</strong></h3><ul><li><p>If the applicant has a <strong>business name</strong>, enter it into Salesforce.</p></li><li><p>If no business name is provided, use the <strong>applicant's full name</strong> as the account name.</p></li><li><p>Enter any available business details, including:</p><ul><li><p>Business Name</p></li><li><p>Phone Number</p></li><li><p>Address</p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>3.3 Create Contact for the Individual</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Enter personal details:</p><ul><li><p>First and Last Name</p></li><li><p>Title (if applicable)</p></li><li><p>SSN</p></li><li><p>License Number (if available)</p></li><li><p>Email</p></li><li><p>Mobile Phone Number</p></li><li><p>Address</p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>3.4 Create Contacts for Additional Guarantors</strong></h3><ul><li><p>If multiple businesses or guarantors are involved, <strong>create a separate contact</strong> for each.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>4. Creating an Opportunity in Salesforce</strong></h2><h3><strong>4.1 Create New Opportunity</strong></h3><ul><li><p>If the applicant meets credit criteria, create a <strong>new opportunity</strong> in Salesforce.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>4.2 Enter Opportunity Details</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Record Type (</strong>TR for Trucking or BL for Business Lending)</p></li><li><p><strong>Customer&rsquo;s Name</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Dealer&rsquo;s Name</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Broker&rsquo;s Name</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Price (from invoice)</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Referral Source Contact</strong></p></li></ul><h3><strong>4.3 Add Opportunity Contact Roles</strong></h3><ul><li><p>This is needed for Credit Decision process - relating the Contacts allows us to get their Personal Credit Reports.</p></li><li><p>Flag relevant Contact Role as IsPrimary.</p></li><li><p>This is also needed for the Funding Agreement Contract that is signed as part of this Deal </p></li></ul><h3><strong>4.4 Create related Vehicle record </strong></h3><p>This represents Collateral that is being used in the deal. </p><p><em>Note: object is named Vehicle due to starting only in Trucking funding where Vehicles were used as Collateral but will generalize to Collateral in the future for other Deal types and other types of Collateral.</em></p><ul><li><p>Input:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Year</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Make</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Model</strong></p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>4.5 Upload Supporting Documents</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Attach the following documents to the <strong>Opportunity record</strong>:</p><ul><li><p>Credit application</p></li><li><p>Bank statements</p></li><li><p>Tax returns</p></li><li><p>Any additional financial documents</p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>4.6 Change Opportunity Stage</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Update the opportunity stage to <strong>&quot;Pending Approval&quot;</strong> with the current date.</p></li><li><p>This action places the opportunity into the <strong>credit team&rsquo;s review queue</strong>.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>5. Next Steps</strong></h2><ul><li><p><strong>Credit Team Review</strong>: The credit team evaluates the opportunity and makes an approval decision.</p></li><li><p><strong>Final Documentation and Funding</strong>: If approved, documentation is finalized, and funding is processed.</p></li></ul>
