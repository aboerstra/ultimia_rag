<h2><strong>1. Initial Setup and Workflow</strong></h2><h3><strong>Trucking (TR) &ndash; A Salesforce-Driven Process</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Highly structured and systematized workflow</strong> using Salesforce.</p></li><li><p><strong>Scalability-focused</strong> setup, allowing for automation and efficiency.</p></li><li><p><strong>Application data is entered directly into Salesforce as a Lead.</strong></p></li><li><p>Uses <strong>credit scoring models</strong> to streamline deal approvals.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Business Lending (BL) &ndash; A Distinct, More Manual Workflow</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Different workflow than TR</strong>, relying more on traditional document-based processing.</p></li><li><p><strong>Less Salesforce-dependent</strong>, with more reliance on external systems like Orion.</p></li><li><p><strong>Product structures differ significantly</strong> from TR due to broader collateral options.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>2. Systems and Tools Used</strong></h2><h3><strong>Trucking (TR) &ndash; Heavy Reliance on Salesforce</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Salesforce is central</strong> for managing applications, approvals, credit scoring, asset tracking, and collections.</p></li><li><p>Uses <strong>LeaseWorks for lease-related data processing</strong> (manual input required).</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Business Lending (BL) &ndash; More Distributed Systems</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Less dependent on Salesforce</strong>, leading to <strong>manual work and inefficiencies</strong>.</p></li><li><p>Uses <strong>Orion for servicing, billing, and collections</strong> (unlike TR, which manages collections in-house).</p></li><li><p><strong>Lacks full integration with LeaseWorks</strong>, requiring manual data entry.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>3. Credit Scoring and Approval</strong></h2><h3><strong>Trucking (TR) &ndash; Uses a Defined Credit Scoring Model</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>TransUnion credit checks</strong> are run on <strong>all customers</strong>, even outside California.</p></li><li><p><strong>Credit scoring model in place</strong> to standardize approvals.</p></li><li><p><strong>More automated decision-making based on predefined scoring rules.</strong></p></li></ul><h3><strong>Business Lending (BL) &ndash; No Standard Credit Scoring Yet</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Currently lacks a formal credit scoring model</strong> (under development).</p></li><li><p><strong>Approval is based more on manual credit report reviews</strong> rather than predefined scoring.</p></li><li><p><strong>Deals are more individualized</strong>, often requiring a deeper review of borrower history.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>4. Collateral Differences</strong></h2><h3><strong>Trucking (TR) &ndash; More Homogeneous Collateral</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Collateral is <strong>standardized (trucks and trailers)</strong>, making it easier to integrate into Salesforce.</p></li><li><p><strong>Vehicles can be tracked using GPS systems</strong> integrated with Salesforce.</p></li><li><p><strong>Resale market is predictable</strong> for repossessed vehicles.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Business Lending (BL) &ndash; Diverse Collateral Portfolio</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Broad range of collateral types</strong>, including:</p><ul><li><p>Non-trucking equipment</p></li><li><p>Real estate</p></li><li><p>Agricultural and construction equipment</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>Real estate deals are more complex</strong>, requiring:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Appraisals</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Property documentation reviews</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Personal financial statements</strong></p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>No standardized collateral management system</strong>, making valuation and tracking more difficult than TR.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>5. Deal Structure Differences</strong></h2><h3><strong>Business Lending (BL) &ndash; More Flexible Structures</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Larger deals</strong>, especially real estate transactions, may offer <strong>interest-only options</strong> for borrowers.</p></li><li><p>More <strong>customized repayment structures</strong>, compared to the <strong>fixed terms in TR</strong>.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Trucking (TR) &ndash; Standardized Deal Structuring</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Deals are <strong>more straightforward</strong>, with consistent terms and financing structures.</p></li><li><p><strong>Standard loan terms and repayment schedules</strong> across deals.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>6. Collections and Asset Management</strong></h2><h3><strong>Trucking (TR) &ndash; In-House Collections and Asset Management</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Collections are managed internally</strong>, with a structured process.</p></li><li><p><strong>Repossession and resale process:</strong></p><ul><li><p>Customers have <strong>10 days</strong> to become current before a vehicle is resold.</p></li><li><p><strong>Salesforce is used to track repossessions</strong> and manage vehicle data.</p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>Business Lending (BL) &ndash; Outsourced Billing and Collections</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Billing and collections are handled by an external company.</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Less internal control over collections</strong>, requiring coordination with third-party services.</p></li><li><p><strong>More complexity in asset management</strong>, especially for real estate deals.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>7. Data Submission &amp; Documentation Differences</strong></h2><h3><strong>Trucking (TR) &ndash; Salesforce-Centric Data Entry</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Application data is entered as a Lead in Salesforce</strong>, creating a structured process.</p></li><li><p>Uses <strong>predefined templates</strong> for documentation, making approvals more efficient.</p></li><li><p><strong>Standardized Power of Attorney instructions</strong> based on customer state.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Business Lending (BL) &ndash; More Manual Application Handling</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Application reception varies</strong>, and data entry is <strong>not standardized in Salesforce</strong>.</p></li><li><p><strong>More manual document processing</strong>, requiring individual review for each deal.</p></li><li><p><strong>Lack of uniform documentation templates</strong>, leading to inconsistencies.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>8.
