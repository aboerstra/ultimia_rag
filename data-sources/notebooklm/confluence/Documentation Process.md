<ac:structured-macro ac:name="toc" ac:schema-version="1" data-layout="default" ac:local-id="70ed86a8-d05e-4a5b-8d48-cd7e79f4ac91" ac:macro-id="38052397-1d4a-4acc-805a-e969193d6551"><ac:parameter ac:name="minLevel">1</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="maxLevel">2</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="outline">false</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="style">none</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="type">list</ac:parameter><ac:parameter ac:name="printable">true</ac:parameter></ac:structured-macro><h2>Explanation of Process &amp; Key Details</h2><p>If we decide to proceed with the deal, we move to the Documentation Process which is a list of documentation for the Dealer/Broker/Applicant to provide such as proof of SSN, details or questions such as to confirm who will handle the title and requests to initiate processes that will need to fund/book the deal such as to initiate an inspection of the truck. This process is referenced as &ldquo;Stipulations to Clear&rdquo;.</p><h2><strong>1. Transition from Application to Booking</strong></h2><ul><li><p>The <strong>documentation process begins</strong> after the <strong>Broker/Dealer/Applicant confirms acceptance of approval terms.</strong></p></li><li><p>The <strong>documentation team</strong> ensures that all required documents are <strong>collected, verified, and processed</strong> before moving to the <strong>booking stage</strong>.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>2. Requesting Documents</strong></h2><h3><strong>2.1 Initiating Document Requests</strong></h3><ul><li><p>After the applicant <strong>accepts the approval terms</strong>, the process of requesting documents begins. User sends email &ldquo;DOCUMENT STIPS TO CLEAR&rdquo; and requests documents from:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Brokers</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Dealers</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Direct Customers (Applicants)</strong></p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>2.2 Receiving Requests</strong></h3><ul><li><p>All document requests are via (and returned to) the designated email address: <a href="mailto:docs@maxmcccc.com"><strong>docs@maxmcc.com</strong></a>.</p></li><li><p>Each request is <strong>reviewed</strong> to ensure it meets documentation requirements <strong>before processing begins</strong>.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>3. Document Handling and Storage</strong></h2><h3><strong>3.1 File Download and Naming</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Documents received via email are:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Downloaded</strong> and stored individually.</p></li><li><p><strong>Reviewed</strong> for completeness.</p></li><li><p><strong>Named using a consistent file naming convention</strong>.</p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>3.2 Storage in Box (Cloud System)</strong></h3><ul><li><p>A <strong>new folder is created in Box</strong> for each document request.</p></li><li><p>Documents are <strong>manually uploaded</strong> to Box.</p></li><li><p>Box serves as the <strong>primary storage system</strong>, as Salesforce storage costs are high.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>4. Verification of Information</strong></h2><p>Before proceeding with documentation, <strong>all critical information must be verified</strong>:</p><h3><strong>4.1 Customer Account Details</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Verify the <strong>customer&rsquo;s name, address, and Social Security Number (SSN)</strong> against the application.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>4.2 Vehicle Details</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Ensure accuracy of the <strong>Vehicle Identification Number (VIN), year, make, model, and mileage</strong>.</p></li><li><p>Compare vehicle details <strong>against the title</strong> to ensure correct information.</p></li><li><p>Obtain a <strong>copy of the vehicle title</strong> from the broker or dealer.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>4.3 Contact Roles Verification</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Update contact roles</strong> on the opportunity, including:</p><ul><li><p><strong>References</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Guarantors</strong></p></li></ul></li><li><p>Ensure that the <strong>&quot;Company Guarantees&quot;</strong> field is <strong>accurate and complete</strong>.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>4.4 Document Review &amp; Verification</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The <strong>funding team downloads</strong> and reviews all attached documents to ensure they meet funding requirements.</p></li><li><p>Any <strong>documents already in Salesforce</strong> are verified, and any missing documents are added to the Opportunity.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>5. Document Generation and Merging</strong></h2><h3><strong>5.1 Automatic Document Generation</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Once verification is complete, the documentation process is <strong>initiated in Salesforce</strong> by clicking a <strong>Document Merge button</strong> on the Opportunity. </p></li><li><p>Documents are <strong>merged into a single file</strong> using <strong>Conga Composer</strong>.</p></li><li><p>The system <strong>automatically generates required templates</strong> based on predefined settings.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>5.2 Document Merging and Editing</strong></h3><ul><li><p>User downloads single document</p></li><li><p><strong>Adobe Acrobat Pro DC</strong> is used to:</p><ul><li><p><strong>Rearrange pages</strong> to ensure correct order.</p></li><li><p><strong>Remove unnecessary documents</strong> or <strong>guarantor sections</strong> based on contact roles.</p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>5.3 Updating fields in Salesforce</strong></h3><ul><li><p>To <strong>track </strong>which documents and information has been provided out of the full list of stipulations, users input/update &ldquo;Funding Checklist&rdquo; fields on the Opportunity.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>6. Funding Checklist</strong></h2><h3><strong>6.1 Purpose of the Funding Checklist</strong></h3><ul><li><p>A <strong>funding checklist</strong> is generated to <strong>track these documentation stipulations/items for funding</strong>.</p></li><li><p>The checklist varies based on:</p><ul><li><p><strong>State regulations</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Vehicle type</strong></p></li><li><p><strong>Financing type</strong> (EFA &ndash; Equipment Finance Agreement vs. Lease)</p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>6.2 Responsibility for Checklist Accuracy</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The <strong>person preparing the documents</strong> ensures the checklist is complete and accurate.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>6.3 Sending the Funding Checklist</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The <strong>rep clicks the &quot;Email Funding Checklist&quot; button</strong> in Salesforce.</p></li><li><p>The system generates a <strong>pre-filled funding checklist</strong> for review.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>6.4 Finalizing the Funding Package</strong></h3><ul><li><p>As Dealers/Brokers/Applicants send in items in the funding checklist, Users repeat process to <strong>input/update &ldquo;Funding Checklist&rdquo; fields</strong> on the Opportunity.</p></li><li><p>The rep <strong>repeats sending the Email Funding Checklist to</strong> <strong>communicate with the Broker/Dealer and </strong>obtain any missing information.</p></li><li><p>Once all requirements are met, the <strong>finalized checklist is sent back via email.</strong></p></li><li><p>Once all items are completed, we can move onto Docs In &amp; Signature stage.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>7. GPS Requirement</strong></h2><p>Ping GPS &amp; attach location of Vehicle into Opportunity *this takes place anywhere from when the Docs go out to Funding unless Dealer doesn't allow installation of GPS until after Funding in which case this is done after Funding</p><h3><strong>7.1 GPS Requirement</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>GPS installation is mandatory</strong> for every financed vehicle.</p></li><li><p>The GPS process typically occurs <strong>before funding</strong> unless the <strong>dealer requires installation after funding</strong>.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>7.2 GPS Location Verification</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The vehicle&rsquo;s <strong>GPS location is pinged</strong>, and its <strong>location is attached to the Opportunity</strong> in Salesforce.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>8. Document Delivery and Signature Process</strong></h2><h3><strong>8.1 Sending Documents for Signature</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Once documents are prepared, they are <strong>sent to the customer for signature</strong>.</p></li><li><p>A <strong>funding checklist is also sent to the broker/dealer</strong>.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>8.2 DocuSign Integration</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Documents are sent via <strong>DocuSign</strong> directly from Salesforce by clicking a <strong>specific button (currently SEND DOCUSIGN)</strong>.</p></li><li><p>A <strong>PDF copy</strong> of the signed documents and funding checklist is also <strong>emailed</strong> to relevant parties.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>8.3 Special Cases &ndash; Power of Attorney</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Power of Attorney (POA) documents cannot be signed via DocuSign</strong> because they require <strong>notarization</strong>.</p></li><li><p>POA documents must be <strong>signed and scanned manually</strong>.</p></li><li><p><ac:emoticon ac:name="warning" ac:emoji-shortname=":warning:" ac:emoji-id="atlassian-warning" ac:emoji-fallback=":warning:" /> roadmap item: include Docusign&rsquo;s e-notorize feature.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>8.4 Identity Verification in DocuSign</strong></h3><ul><li><p><strong>Knowledge-Based Authentication (KBA)</strong> is used in <strong>DocuSign</strong> to <strong>verify the customer's identity</strong> before signing.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>8.5 eVault</strong></h3><ul><li><p>Documents returned to Docusign are stored in an integrated eVault / eOriginal.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>Manual Processes and Inefficiencies</strong></h2><h3><strong>Manual Steps in Documentation</strong></h3><ul><li><p>The documentation process remains <strong>highly manual</strong>, causing delays.</p></li><li><p><strong>Reps must manually:</strong></p><ul><li><p>Remove <strong>unnecessary documents</strong> before sending final copies.</p></li><li><p>Adjust document <strong>layouts and sections</strong>.</p></li><li><p><strong>Manually move documents</strong> within the contract package.</p></li></ul></li></ul><h3><strong>Missing Automation Features</strong></h3><ul><li><p>There is <strong>no built-in documentation checklist</strong> in Salesforce, leading to inconsistencies.</p></li><li><p>The <strong>lack of proper automation</strong> means staff <strong>manually format documents</strong> rather than using predefined logic.</p></li></ul><h3><strong>Back-and-Forth Communication Delays</strong></h3><ul><li><p>A significant portion of time is spent <strong>exchanging emails with brokers</strong> to finalize documentation.</p></li><li><p>Before documents can be processed, there are often <strong>multiple revisions and requests for missing information</strong>.</p></li><li><p>This <strong>extends the total processing time</strong> for each deal.</p></li></ul><hr /><h2><strong>Summary of Key Challenges &amp; Improvement Opportunities</strong></h2><table data-table-width="760" data-layout="default"
